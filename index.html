Common Questions

Florida Insurance FAQs

What does it mean to be an independent agent? +
An independent agent isn't locked to a single insurance company. We work with 30+ carriers and shop your policy to all of them simultaneously. That competition drives your price down — unlike a captive agent (State Farm, Allstate, etc.) who can only offer you one company's rates.
Is Florida coastal / windstorm insurance hard to find? +
It can be — many national carriers have pulled back from Florida's coastal market. But we specialize exactly in this area. We have carrier relationships specifically for high-wind zones, coastal properties, and difficult-to-insure homes that other agents struggle to place.
How fast can I get a quote? +
Most standard quotes come back same business day. For complex commercial or coastal properties, usually within 24-48 hours. If you have a closing deadline, tell us upfront — we can often accommodate rush requests.
Do I need flood insurance in Florida? +
If you're in a FEMA-designated high-risk flood zone (AE, VE, etc.) and have a mortgage, it's typically required. But even outside high-risk zones, Florida's geography means flood risk is real — and homeowners insurance never covers flood damage. We can compare NFIP vs. private flood options to find the best deal.
Can you help mortgage officers and realtors with their clients? +
Absolutely — this is a core part of what we do. We communicate directly with lenders, provide binders and declarations pages fast, and make sure nothing holds up a closing. We'd love to be your go-to insurance resource for your clients.
What counties do you NOT serve? +
We currently serve 65 of 67 Florida counties. The only exception is Manatee County. If you're in any other Florida county, we're ready to help you.
